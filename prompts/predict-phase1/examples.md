# Worked Examples

Book-agnostic examples showing the 5 transforms.

## Example 1: Simple Narrative (Ruth 1:2)

### NIV
> The man's name was Elimelek, his wife's name was Naomi, and the names of his two sons were Mahlon and Kilion. They were Ephrathites from Bethlehem, Judah. And they went to Moab and lived there.

### Transforms

| Step | Text |
|------|------|
| 1️⃣ Copy | The man's name was Elimelek, his wife's name was Naomi, and the names of his two sons were Mahlon and Kilion. They were Ephrathites from Bethlehem, Judah. And they went to Moab and lived there. |
| 2️⃣ Pronouns | The man's name was Elimelek. His wife's name was Naomi. The names of his 2 sons were Mahlon and Kilion. The man, his wife, and his 2 sons were Ephrathites from Bethlehem, Judah. And the man, his wife, and his 2 sons went to the country of Moab and lived in the country of Moab. |
| 3️⃣ Vocabulary | That man's name was Elimelech. That man's wife's name was Naomi. One son's name was Mahlon. The other son's name was Kilion. Elimelech and Naomi were people in Ephrah's family in the town named Bethlehem in the region named Judah. That family went to a country named Moab and lived in Moab. |
| 4️⃣ Group | That man's name was Elimelech. That man's wife's name was Naomi. One son's name was Mahlon. The other son's name was Kilion. Elimelech and Naomi were in Ephrah's family/clan. That family was living in a town named Bethlehem in the region named Judah. That family went to a country named Moab. And that family lived in Moab. |
| 5️⃣ Clauses | That man's name was Elimelech. And that man's wife's name was Naomi. One son's name was Mahlon. And the other son's name was Kilion. Elimelech and Naomi were in Ephrah's family/clan. That family was living in a town named Bethlehem [which was in Judah]. But that family went to a country named Moab. And that family lived in Moab. |

**Key transforms:**
- "They" → explicit subjects
- "two" → "2"
- "Ephrathites from Bethlehem, Judah" → family/clan + town + region
- Relative clause for location

## Example 2: Death & Consequence (Ruth 1:3)

### NIV
> Now Elimelek, Naomi's husband, died, and she was left with her two sons.

### Transforms

| Step | Text |
|------|------|
| 1️⃣ Copy | Now Elimelek, Naomi's husband, died, and she was left with her two sons. |
| 2️⃣ Pronouns | Elimelek, Naomi's husband, died. Naomi was left with Naomi's 2 sons. |
| 3️⃣ Vocabulary | Elimelech, Naomi's husband, died. Naomi lived with only Naomi's 2 sons. |
| 4️⃣ Group | (no grouping needed) |
| 5️⃣ Clauses | Elimelech [who was Naomi's husband] died later. So Naomi lived with only Naomi's 2 sons. |

**Key transforms:**
- Apposition → relative clause
- "was left with" → "lived with only"
- "she" → "Naomi"
- "and" → "So" (consequence)
- Added "later" (temporal)

## Example 3: Command with Quote (Genesis 22:2)

### NIV
> Then God said, "Take your son, your only son, whom you love—Isaac—and go to the region of Moriah."

### Transforms

| Step | Text |
|------|------|
| 1️⃣ Copy | Then God said, "Take your son, your only son, whom you love—Isaac—and go to the region of Moriah." |
| 2️⃣ Pronouns | Then God said to Abraham, "You(Abraham) take your(Abraham's) son, your(Abraham's) only son, [whom you(Abraham) love]—Isaac—and you(Abraham) go to the region of Moriah." |
| 3️⃣ Vocabulary | Then God said to Abraham, "You(Abraham) (imp) take your(Abraham's) son. You(Abraham) have only 1 son. You(Abraham) love that son. That son is Isaac. You(Abraham) (imp) go to the region named Moriah." |
| 4️⃣ Group | (split into clearer sentences) |
| 5️⃣ Clauses | Then God said, ["You(Abraham) (imp) take your(Abraham's) son [who is your(Abraham's) only son] [whom you(Abraham) love]. That son is named Isaac. And you(Abraham) (imp) go to a region named Moriah."] |

**Key transforms:**
- Implicit listener made explicit
- Apposition → relative clauses
- "your only son" → relative clause
- Commands with (imp)
- Quote structure with brackets

## Example 4: Blessing/Prayer (Ruth 2:4)

### NIV
> Just then Boaz arrived from Bethlehem and greeted the harvesters, "The Lord be with you!" "The Lord bless you!" they answered.

### Transforms

| Step | Text |
|------|------|
| 1️⃣ Copy | Just then Boaz arrived from Bethlehem and greeted the harvesters, "The Lord be with you!" "The Lord bless you!" they answered. |
| 2️⃣ Pronouns | Then Boaz came from Bethlehem. Boaz greeted the workers. Boaz said, "The Lord be with you(workers)!" The workers answered, "The Lord bless you(Boaz)!" |
| 3️⃣ Vocabulary | Then Boaz came from a town named Bethlehem. Boaz greeted the servants/workers. Boaz said, "I(Boaz) pray [that Yahweh will be with you(workers)]." The servants/workers answered, "I(workers) pray [that Yahweh will do good things for you(Boaz)]." |
| 4️⃣ Group | (no grouping needed) |
| 5️⃣ Clauses | Then Boaz came from a town named Bethlehem. Boaz greeted the servants/workers. Boaz said, ["I(Boaz) pray [that Yahweh will be with you(workers)]"]. The servants/workers answered, ["We(workers) pray [that Yahweh will do good things for you(Boaz)]"]. |

**Key transforms:**
- "arrived" → "came" (verb issue)
- "harvesters" → "servants/workers" (L2 pairing)
- "The Lord be with you" → pray-hope construction
- "bless" → "do good things for"
- Quote structure

## Example 5: Rhetorical Question (Romans 8:31)

### NIV
> What, then, shall we say in response to these things? If God is for us, who can be against us?

### Transforms

| Step | Text |
|------|------|
| 1️⃣ Copy | What, then, shall we say in response to these things? If God is for us, who can be against us? |
| 2️⃣ Pronouns | Which thing will we(Paul) _excl say about these things? [If God is for us(Paul) _excl] which person is able [to be against us(Paul) _excl]? |
| 3️⃣ Vocabulary | Which thing will we(Paul) _excl say about these things-B? [If-A God helps us(Paul) _excl] which person is able [to fight against us(Paul) _excl]? |
| 4️⃣ Group | (no grouping needed) |
| 5️⃣ Clauses | (yesrhetorical) Which thing will we(Paul) _excl say about these things-B? [If-A God helps us(Paul) _excl] which person is able [to fight against us(Paul) _excl]? (statement) We(Paul) _excl can say good things about these things-B. [If-A God helps us(Paul) _excl] no person is able [to fight against us(Paul) _excl]. |

**Key transforms:**
- "What" → "which thing"
- "can" → "is able [to...]"
- "for us" → "helps us"
- "against us" → "fight against us"
- Rhetorical question + statement version
- Exclusive "we" marked

## Pattern Summary

| NIV Pattern | He1 Transform |
|-------------|---------------|
| Third person pronoun | Resolve to noun |
| Apposition (X, Y's Z) | Relative clause [who was Y's Z] |
| Nationality (X the Y-ite) | Relative clause [who was from Y] |
| "was left with" | "lived with only" |
| "The Lord be with you" | "I pray [that Yahweh will be with you]" |
| "bless" | "do good things for" |
| "can" | "is able [to...]" |
| Rhetorical question | Add (statement) version |
| Number words | Digits |
